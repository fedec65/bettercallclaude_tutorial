# Scenario 6.2: Litigation Preparation

> **Comprehensive case preparation from intake to strategy**

**⏱️ Estimated time: 45 minutes**

---

## The Situation

Your client calls with an urgent matter:

**Client message:**
> "We need to sue ABC Corp. They breached our distribution agreement—they started selling directly to our Swiss customers, undercutting our prices. We've lost significant revenue. What are our options?"

---

## Step-by-Step Walkthrough

### Step 1: Structured Intake (10 min)

**Type:**
```
/briefing Client wants to sue for breach of exclusive distribution agreement. Competitor (ABC Corp) started direct sales in Switzerland, undercutting prices. Need to assess options: damages, injunction, or both.
```

**Panel questions you'll answer:**
1. What is the duration and territory of the exclusivity?
2. What evidence do you have of direct sales?
3. What are the quantified damages?
4. Is there a jurisdiction clause in the agreement?
5. What is the relationship value—can it be preserved?

### Step 2: Document and Evidence Review (5 min)

Gather and analyze:

**Type:**
```
/analyze [distribution agreement] for:
1. Exclusivity clause terms
2. Territory definition
3. Breach consequences
4. Jurisdiction and governing law
5. Termination provisions
```

### Step 3: Legal Research (15 min)

**3.1 Research Precedents:**
```
/research exclusive distribution agreement breach Switzerland direct sales undercutting
```

**3.2 Research Damages Calculation:**
```
/research lost profits calculation contractual breach Switzerland distribution agreements
```

**3.3 Research Injunctive Relief:**
```
/research preliminary injunction requirements Switzerland commercial disputes
```

### Step 4: Strategy Assessment (10 min)

**4.1 Build Your case:**
```
/strategy Breach of exclusive distribution agreement claim:
- Clear exclusivity clause in writing
- Evidence of direct sales (screenshots, customer complaints)
- Documented price undercutting
- Quantified revenue loss: CHF 500,000 over 6 months
- Seeking damages + injunction
```

**4.2 Challenge your own case:**
```
/adversary Our position: ABC Corp's direct sales violated exclusivity clause, causing CHF 500,000 in lost profits. We have written agreement and customer evidence.
```

**What to watch for:**
- Weak points in your evidence
- Counter-arguments ABC will raise
- Risks of litigation vs. settlement value

### Step 5: Client Options Memo (15 min)

**Type:**
```
/draft litigation strategy memo for client including:
1. Case assessment (probability of success)
2. Damages analysis
3. Injunctive relief prospects
4. Cost-benefit of litigation vs. settlement
5. Recommended next steps with timeline
```

---

## Decision Tree for Litigation

```
                        ┌── Evidence Strong? (Documented direct sales, price comparison)
                        │
                ┌── YES ──┤ Evidence Weak? (Rumors, no documentation)
                │           │
        ┌── Damages Clear? │           └── Consider: Demand letter first, gather evidence
        │   (CHF 500K documented)
        │
        │
        │   └── NO ──→ Consider: Build damages case before filing
        │
        │
        └── NO ──→ Consider: Settlement-focused approach
        │
        │
        └── Relationship Value?
            │
            ├── High ──→ Consider: Negotiated resolution
            │
            └── Low ──→ Consider: Aggressive litigation
```

---

## What to Validate Before Proceeding

### Legal Standing
- [ ] Clear contractual breach
- [ ] Documented damages (not speculative)
- [ ] Proper jurisdiction identified

### Evidence Quality
- [ ] Direct sales documented
- [ ] Price undercutting evidenced
- [ ] Customer diversion shown

### Strategic Assessment
- [ ] Probability of success estimated
- [ ] Cost-benefit analyzed
- [ ] Client risk tolerance understood

### Alternative Options
- [ ] Demand letter as first step considered
- [ ] Settlement potential assessed
- [ ] Relationship preservation weighed
