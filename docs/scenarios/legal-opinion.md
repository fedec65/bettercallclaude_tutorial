# Scenario 6.3: Legal Opinion

> **From research to polished opinion**

**⏱️ Estimated time: 45 minutes**

---

## The Situation

Your client needs a formal legal opinion on a regulatory compliance question.

**Client email:**
> "We're expanding our fintech app to offer payment services. Can you give us a legal opinion on whether we need a fintech license? We need this for our board meeting in 2 weeks."

---

## Step-by-Step Walkthrough

### Step 1: Scope Definition (5 min)

**Type:**
```
/briefing Legal opinion needed: Does our fintech app require a FINMA license under FinSA/FINIG?
App offers: [describe features]
Target market: Switzerland
Opinion needed for: Board meeting
Format: Formal legal opinion with citations
```

### Step 2: Regulatory Framework Research (15 min)

**2.1 Research FinSA/FINIG scope:**
```
/research FINMA license requirement fintech payment services Switzerland FinIG
```

**2.2 Research comparable cases:**
```
/research fintech license exemptions Switzerland small payment services
```

**2.3 Research sandbox provisions:**
```
/research FINMA regulatory sandbox fintech Switzerland 2024
```

### Step 3: Apply Law to Facts (10 min)

**Type:**
```
/strategy Does [specific app features] trigger FinIG license requirement?
App facts: [detailed feature list]
Regulatory framework: FinIG Art. [relevant articles]
Exemptions considered: [list relevant exemptions]
```

### Step 4: Challenge Your Conclusion (5 min)

**Type:**
```
/adversary Our conclusion: [state your conclusion, e.g., "No license required due to sandbox exemption"]

Challenge this finding. What would FINMA argue?
```

**Watch for:**
- Overbroad interpretation of exemptions
- Missing regulatory concerns
- Recent enforcement actions

### Step 5: Draft the Opinion (20 min)

**Type:**
```
/draft formal legal opinion for board of directors on FINMA licensing requirement.

Include:
1. Executive summary with clear conclusion
2. Factual background
3. Legal framework analysis
4. Application to facts
5. Risk assessment
6. Recommendations
7. Disclaimer

Cite: All relevant statutory provisions and key precedents
Format: Professional legal opinion with proper structure
```

### Step 6: Citation Verification (5 min)

**Type:**
```
/cite [each BGE and statutory citation in your opinion]
```

**Verify:**
- All citations exist and are accurate
- Quotes are correct
- References are properly formatted

---

## Legal Opinion Structure

```markdown
# LEGAL OPINION

**To**: Board of Directors, [Company]
**From**: [Your Name], Attorney-at-Law
**Date**: [Date]
**Re**: FINMA Licensing Requirement for [App Name]

---

## 1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

**Conclusion**: [Clear yes/no/conditional answer]

**Key Reasoning**: [2-3 sentences]

**Recommendation**: [What client should do]

---

## 2. BACKGROUND

### 2.1 Facts
[Detailed factual background]

### 2.2 Question Presented
[Exact legal question asked]

---

## 3. LEGAL FRAMEWORK

### 3.1 Statutory Basis
- **FinIG Art. X**: [text and relevance]
- **FinSA Art. Y**: [text and relevance]

### 3.2 Regulatory Guidance
- **FINMA Circular [X]**: [relevance]

### 3.3 Relevant Precedents
- **BGE [citation]**: [holding and relevance]

---

## 4. ANALYSIS

### 4.1 [Issue 1]
[Analysis]

### 4.2 [Issue 2]
[Analysis]

---

## 5. CONCLUSIONS

[Clear legal conclusions]

---

## 6. RECOMMENDATIONS

1. [Recommendation 1]
2. [Recommendation 2]

---

## 7. DISCLAIMER

This opinion is based on the facts as presented and the law as of [date]. [Standard disclaimer language]
```

---

## Quality Assurance Checklist

### Content Quality
- [ ] Conclusion directly answers the question asked
- [ ] All material facts are addressed
- [ ] Legal framework is complete
- [ ] Analysis connects facts to law

### Citation Integrity
- [ ] All statutory citations verified
- [ ] All case citations verified
- [ ] No hallucinated citations

### Professional Standards
- [ ] Appropriate disclaimers included
- [ ] Limitations clearly stated
- [ ] Practical recommendations provided

### Risk Assessment
- [ ] Regulatory risks identified
- [ ] Enforcement risks assessed
- [ ] Mitigation steps recommended

---

> ⚠️ **Uncertain about your conclusion?**
> - It's better to say "likely" than to be wrong
> - Consider adding conditional analysis ("If X, then Y")
> - Flag areas requiring additional specialist input (e.g., "FINMA should be consulted on Z")
> - A nuanced opinion beats an overconfident one
